# CIA-PPC-META Skill
*Cover Story + Asset Recruitment + Terrain Mapping — applied to Paid Advertising*

## Framework 1: Cover Story — Ad Positioning from the Buyer's Mission

The #1 reason ads fail: they lead with the product, not the mission.

CIA officers don't open with "I'm a CIA officer." They open with what matters to the target.

**Bad ad (leads with product):**
> "Padzilla — Giant Interactive Displays for Business. Shop Now."

**CIA Cover Story ad (leads with mission):**
> "Your lobby should stop people cold. See what 4,000 businesses already know."

### The Cover Story Ad Brief

For every ad, answer these before writing a word:

```
MISSION: What is the buyer trying to accomplish? (Not: what does your product do)
LEGEND: Who are they? What triggered this search/scroll TODAY?
COVER: How does your ad speak their language, not yours?
ASSET: What do they get when they click? (Must match the mission exactly)
EXTRACTION: One action. No options.
```

### Cover Story by Ad Type

**Google Search Ads (High Intent — Decision Stage)**
- Searcher has a mission. Your ad is the answer.
- Headline 1: Mirror the search intent exactly
- Headline 2: Your unique proof point
- Headline 3: Remove the top objection
- Description: Mission outcome + CTA

```
Example (SEO Services):
H1: "SEO Services in Orlando, FL"          ← Mirror their search
H2: "Ranking Results in 90 Days or Less"   ← Proof point
H3: "No Long-Term Contracts Required"      ← Disarm top objection
DESC: "We've helped 50+ local businesses reach page one. 
       Free audit shows exactly where you're losing traffic."
```

**Meta/Instagram Ads (Interruption — Awareness/Consideration)**
- Viewer has NO mission. YOU create the mission.
- Hook (first 3 seconds): Create awareness of a problem they didn't know they had
- Body: Amplify the problem + introduce the solution
- CTA: Low-friction next step (not "Buy Now" — "Learn More" / "See How")

```
Example (Padzilla — Hotel GMs):
HOOK: "Why are guests walking past your lobby without stopping?"
BODY: "Your competitors just installed interactive display walls.
       Guests are photographing them. Tagging them. Coming back for them.
       Padzilla turns dead lobby space into your best marketing asset."
CTA: "See it in action →"
```

## Framework 2: Asset Recruitment — Audience Funnel Sequencing

Never ask for the full commitment from a stranger.

CIA asset recruitment applied to paid media:

```
COLD AUDIENCE (Strangers)
  ↓ Stage: SPOTTING
  ↓ Goal: Awareness — make them aware of a problem or aspiration
  ↓ Platform: Meta broad audiences, YouTube, Display
  ↓ Ask: Watch a video / visit a page / follow
  ↓ Cover: Educational, provocative, identity-based

WARM AUDIENCE (Engaged — visited site, watched 50%+ video, clicked ad)
  ↓ Stage: ASSESSMENT
  ↓ Goal: Consideration — position your solution vs. alternatives
  ↓ Platform: Meta retargeting, Google Display retargeting, YouTube retargeting
  ↓ Ask: Download / sign up for webinar / request info
  ↓ Cover: Comparative, social proof, "here's why us"

HOT AUDIENCE (High intent — visited pricing page, added to cart, requested demo)
  ↓ Stage: DEVELOPMENT
  ↓ Goal: Conversion — remove final objection, make the ask
  ↓ Platform: Google Search, Meta retargeting, direct
  ↓ Ask: Buy / book / schedule / call
  ↓ Cover: Objection disarm, urgency, direct offer

EXISTING CUSTOMERS
  ↓ Stage: HANDLING
  ↓ Goal: Retention, upsell, referral
  ↓ Platform: Meta custom audience, email-matched, YouTube
  ↓ Ask: Review / refer / upgrade
  ↓ Cover: Exclusive, insider, loyalty
```

### Audience Recruitment Campaign Structure

**Google Ads Campaign Structure (CIA Method):**
```
Campaign 1 — SPOTTING (Brand Awareness)
  Ad Group: Problem-aware keywords ("how to improve lobby experience")
  Bid: Maximize clicks / Target impression share
  Cover: Educational angle

Campaign 2 — ASSESSMENT (Consideration)
  Ad Group: Solution-aware keywords ("interactive display for hotels")
  Bid: Target CPA / Maximize conversions
  Cover: Comparative angle

Campaign 3 — DEVELOPMENT (Decision)
  Ad Group: Purchase-ready keywords ("Padzilla price", "buy interactive display")
  Bid: Target ROAS
  Cover: Direct offer + objection disarm

Campaign 4 — RETARGETING (Warm audience)
  Ad Group: Site visitors / Video viewers
  Bid: Target CPA
  Cover: Social proof + urgency
```

**Meta Campaign Structure (CIA Method):**
```
Campaign 1 — COLD (Broad + Lookalike)
  Objective: Reach / Video Views / Traffic
  Audience: Interest targeting + 2-5% lookalike
  Creative: Video hook — problem awareness
  
Campaign 2 — WARM (Website retargeting)
  Objective: Conversions / Lead gen
  Audience: Visited site last 30 days (exclude customers)
  Creative: Social proof + comparison

Campaign 3 — HOT (High-intent retargeting)
  Objective: Conversions
  Audience: Pricing page visitors / cart abandoners (last 14 days)
  Creative: Direct offer + strong CTA + scarcity if real
```

## Framework 3: Terrain Mapping — Competitor Ad Intelligence

Map the battlefield before you spend a dollar.

### The 5-Zone Paid Ad Terrain Map

```
Zone 1: OCCUPIED — What keywords competitors bid on that you don't
Zone 2: CONTESTED — Keywords you're both bidding on (compare ad copy quality)
Zone 3: UNOCCUPIED — High-intent keywords with low competition / low CPCs
Zone 4: FORTIFIED — Brand terms and competitor names they've locked up
Zone 5: SUPPLY LINES — Their weakest ads, worst landing pages, lowest QS signals
```

### Competitor Ad Deconstruction (CIA Intel)

When you see a competitor's ad, analyze it like an intelligence report:

```
1. What MICE driver are they using?
   (Money/ROI angle? Ideology/identity? Coercion/FOMO? Ego/status?)

2. What STAGE are they targeting?
   (Awareness hook? Consideration comparison? Decision offer?)

3. What objection are they disarming?
   (Price? Trust? Speed? Complexity?)

4. What's their CTA — and is it friction-matched to the stage?
   (High-friction CTA on cold audience = rookie mistake)

5. What are they NOT saying?
   (The gap = your opportunity. Own what they're ignoring.)
```

### Terrain Mapping Prompts for PPC

**Competitor ad analysis:**
```
Analyze these competitor ads using CIA Terrain Mapping:
[paste ad copy]

Identify:
- MICE driver they're using
- Recruitment stage they're targeting
- The objection they're disarming
- Their biggest gap (what they're NOT saying)
- How to write a counter-ad that owns the gap
```

**Campaign structure audit:**
```
Audit this Google Ads account structure against CIA Asset Recruitment.
Are cold/warm/hot audiences getting the right covers?
Where is the sequence breaking?
What campaigns are missing?
[paste campaign names/structure]
```

**Unoccupied keyword terrain:**
```
For [niche/product], find Google search terms with:
- Clear purchase intent
- Low competition (few advertisers bidding)
- Mismatched ad copy (ads don't match searcher mission)
These are unoccupied zones. Build ads that own them.
```

## Ad Copy Templates (CIA Method)

### Google Search Ad — AIPDA Format
```
H1: [Mirror the search mission]
H2: [Proof that you deliver the outcome]
H3: [Disarm the #1 objection]
D1: [ANCHOR — Make the problem/opportunity vivid]
D2: [ACTIVATE — One clear next step with low friction]
```

### Meta Ad — Cold Audience
```
HOOK (stop the scroll): [Problem they didn't know they had / aspiration trigger]
LINE 1: [Identify — mirror their situation back]
LINE 2: [Prove — one specific, concrete example]
LINE 3: [Disarm — acknowledge the objection]
CTA: [Low-friction ask — "See how" / "Learn more" / "Watch"]
```

### Meta Ad — Warm Retargeting
```
HOOK: [Reference their awareness — "Still thinking about X?"]
BODY: [Social proof + specific result]
DISARM: [Remove the specific objection that stopped them last time]
CTA: [Higher-friction ask — "Book a demo" / "Get your quote"]
```

## ROAS Diagnosis (CIA Debrief)

When a campaign underperforms, run this debrief:

```
1. MISSION MISMATCH — Does the ad speak the searcher's mission or your product?
2. STAGE MISMATCH — Are you asking cold audiences for hot-audience commitments?
3. TERRAIN BLINDNESS — Are you in occupied territory fighting for keywords you can't win?
4. COVER STORY FAILURE — Does the landing page match the ad's promise exactly?
5. EXTRACTION FAILURE — Is there more than one CTA? (Always kill the extras)
```
