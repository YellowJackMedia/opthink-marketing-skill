# CIA-SEO Skill
*Cover Story + Asset Recruitment + Terrain Mapping — applied to Search Engine Optimization*

## Framework 1: Cover Story — Searcher Intent Positioning

CIA officers never lead with who they are. They lead with what the target needs.

**The SEO mistake most brands make:** They write content about themselves.
**The CIA fix:** Write content about the searcher's problem. Your brand is the cover story. Their outcome is the mission.

### The Cover Story Content Brief

Before writing any page, answer these in order:

```
1. LEGEND (Who is the searcher?)
   - Job title / life situation
   - What triggered this search RIGHT NOW?
   - Emotional state: frustrated / curious / urgent / researching?

2. MISSION (What do they actually need?)
   - Stated need: what they typed
   - Real need: what they actually want to achieve
   - Hidden need: what they're afraid to admit

3. COVER (How your content positions itself)
   - Does the title speak to THEIR mission or YOUR product?
   - Does the first paragraph prove you understand their problem?
   - Is your brand the solution or the guide? (Always guide — never hero)

4. EXTRACTION (The CTA)
   - What's the one next step that serves THEIR mission?
   - Never more than one CTA per page
```

### Cover Story Keyword Classification

Before targeting any keyword, classify it:

| Keyword Type | Searcher Mission | Content Cover |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Awareness** | "Something feels wrong" | Educational — be the expert who names the problem |
| **Consideration** | "What are my options?" | Comparative — be the honest guide |
| **Decision** | "Which one do I pick?" | Persuasive — be the trusted recommendation |
| **Retention** | "How do I get the most out of this?" | Support — be the partner |

**Rule:** Your cover story (content type) must match the searcher's mission exactly. Decision content on an awareness keyword = mission failure.

## Framework 2: Asset Recruitment — Content Funnel SEO

CIA recruits assets in stages. No handler asks for the full commitment on first contact.

**The SEO asset recruitment sequence:**
```
Stage 1: SPOTTING     — Awareness content (blog, informational)
Stage 2: ASSESSMENT   — Consideration content (comparisons, guides)
Stage 3: DEVELOPMENT  — Decision content (case studies, pricing, demos)
Stage 4: HANDLING     — Retention content (how-tos, advanced guides)
Stage 5: TERMINATION  — Win-back / churn prevention content
```

### Content Recruitment Map

```
SPOTTING (Top of Funnel)
  Intent: Informational
  Keywords: "what is X", "how does X work", "why does X happen"
  Goal: Get indexed. Get found. Build trust. Capture email.
  Internal link to: → Assessment content

ASSESSMENT (Middle of Funnel)
  Intent: Navigational / Commercial Investigation
  Keywords: "best X for Y", "X vs Y", "X alternatives", "how to choose X"
  Goal: Establish authority. Introduce your solution naturally.
  Internal link to: → Development content

DEVELOPMENT (Bottom of Funnel)
  Intent: Transactional / Commercial
  Keywords: "[brand] reviews", "X pricing", "hire X consultant", "X services"
  Goal: Convert. Remove objections. Make the ask.
  Internal link to: → Handling content (post-conversion)

HANDLING (Post-Conversion)
  Intent: Navigational (existing customers)
  Keywords: "[brand] login", "how to use X feature", "X tutorial"
  Goal: Retain. Upsell. Turn customers into advocates.
```

### Asset Recruitment Prompts

**Map a content funnel:**
```
Apply CIA Asset Recruitment to this keyword set: [keywords]
Map each keyword to a recruitment stage (Spotting/Assessment/Development/Handling).
Identify gaps in the sequence — where does the funnel break?
Suggest 3 missing content pieces to complete the recruitment chain.
```

**Audit existing content:**
```
Review this site's content structure: [URL or list of pages]
Identify which recruitment stage each page serves.
Find where prospects are being asked to commit too early (skipped stages).
Recommend internal linking changes to fix the sequence.
```

**Write a stage-matched brief:**
```
Write an SEO content brief for a [stage] asset targeting [keyword].
Searcher profile: [description]
Their mission at this stage: [what they need to believe/know to move forward]
Cover story: [how we position without selling]
Next stage CTA: [where they go next]
```

## Framework 3: Terrain Mapping — SEO Competitive Intelligence

CIA maps every threat and asset before any operation.
In SEO: map your competitor's content terrain before writing a single word.

### The 5-Zone Terrain Map

```
Zone 1: OCCUPIED TERRITORY
  - Keywords competitors rank for that you don't
  - Their top pages by organic traffic
  - Content formats they dominate (video, long-form, tools)

Zone 2: CONTESTED TERRITORY  
  - Keywords where you AND competitors rank (positions 1-20)
  - Where you rank 4-15 (winnable with optimization)
  - Head-to-head comparisons

Zone 3: UNOCCUPIED TERRITORY (Your Opportunity)
  - Keywords with search volume that NO competitor ranks for
  - Questions being asked that nobody answers well
  - Emerging topics not yet covered

Zone 4: FORTIFIED POSITIONS
  - Competitor pages with 100+ backlinks (avoid direct combat)
  - Branded searches that are locked
  - SERP features they dominate (featured snippets, PAA boxes)

Zone 5: SUPPLY LINES (Their Weaknesses)
  - Thin content ranking on authority alone
  - Outdated posts not refreshed in 12+ months
  - Topics where their content doesn't match searcher intent
  - Underserved angles on their top keywords
```

### Terrain Map Prompts

**Full terrain analysis:**
```
Conduct a CIA Terrain Map for [my domain] vs [competitor domains].
Map all 5 zones. Prioritize Zone 3 (unoccupied) and Zone 5 (supply lines).
Output: a prioritized list of content opportunities sorted by effort vs. impact.
```

**Supply line attack:**
```
Identify the top 10 pages on [competitor.com] that:
- Rank in positions 1-5
- Have thin content or poor searcher intent match
- Are 12+ months old without updates
These are their supply lines. Build a counter-content plan to take each one.
```

**Unoccupied territory scan:**
```
For the niche [X], find keywords with 100-5,000 monthly searches where:
- No single page has more than 20 referring domains
- The current top results don't fully answer the query
- The topic is adjacent to [my core topic]
These are unoccupied zones. Prioritize by search volume.
```

## SEO Content Production Workflow (CIA Method)

```
Step 1: TERRAIN MAP — Identify zone (occupied/unoccupied/contested)
Step 2: COVER STORY — Define searcher mission + content positioning
Step 3: RECRUITMENT STAGE — Match content to funnel stage
Step 4: BRIEF — Write using Cover Story framework
Step 5: PUBLISH — Internal link to next recruitment stage
Step 6: DEBRIEF — 90 days: Did it rank? Did it recruit? Optimize.
```
